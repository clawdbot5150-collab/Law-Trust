Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Law-Trust.com ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website law-trust.com.

Summary: We collect minimal data (analytics, cookies, contact form submissions). We never sell your data. You have full GDPR and CCPA rights to access, delete, or export your information.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Automatically Collected Information

When you visit law-trust.com, we automatically collect certain information about your device and browsing behavior through cookies and analytics tools:

  • Usage Data: Pages viewed, time spent on pages, referral source, browser type, device type, IP address (anonymized)
  • Analytics: We use Google Analytics (ID: G-K90ZD6K7CH) to understand how visitors use our site
  • Cookies: Small text files stored on your device to improve site functionality and track usage

1.2 Information You Provide

We collect information you voluntarily provide when you:

  • Contact Us: Name, email address, and message content submitted via our contact form
  • Subscribe (if applicable): Email address for newsletters or updates

1.3 Information We Do NOT Collect

We do not collect:

  • Legal documents or personal legal information (we're a comparison site, not a legal service)
  • Financial information or payment details (we don't process payments)
  • Precise geolocation data

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for:

  • Site Improvement: Analyze usage patterns to improve content, navigation, and user experience
  • Communication: Respond to contact form inquiries within 1-2 business days
  • Analytics: Understand which articles are most helpful and which services users are interested in
  • Legal Compliance: Comply with applicable laws and regulations

3. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services that may collect data:

3.1 Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to analyze website traffic. Google may use cookies to collect data about your usage. You can opt out using the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on.

Learn more: Google Privacy Policy

3.2 Affiliate Networks

When you click links to legal document services we review, you may be redirected through affiliate networks (Impact, ShareASale, CJ Affiliate, etc.). These networks use cookies to track referrals. We earn a commission if you purchase a service, but we never share your personal information with these networks.

3.3 Hosting Provider

Our website is hosted on secure servers. Server logs may temporarily store IP addresses for security and performance purposes.

4. Cookies

We use cookies for:

  • Essential cookies: Required for site functionality
  • Analytics cookies: Google Analytics tracking (can be disabled)
  • Affiliate cookies: Track referrals when you click service links (required for commission attribution)

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may limit site functionality.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties.

We may share data only in these limited circumstances:

  • Service Providers: Analytics providers (Google Analytics), hosting providers — only to the extent necessary for service operation
  • Legal Requirements: If required by law, court order, or government regulation
  • Business Transfer: If Law-Trust.com is acquired or merged, data may transfer to the new owner (you will be notified)

6. Data Security

We implement reasonable security measures to protect your information:

  • HTTPS encryption for all site traffic
  • Secure server infrastructure
  • Limited access to contact form submissions (editorial team only)

However, no internet transmission is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Data Retention

  • Contact Form Submissions: Stored for 2 years, then deleted
  • Analytics Data: Google Analytics retains data for 26 months (configurable)
  • Cookies: Expire according to their individual settings (typically 30 days to 2 years)

8. Your Rights (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have these rights under GDPR:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your personal data we hold
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten"): Request deletion of your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Opt out of analytics tracking or communications
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@law-trust.com. We will respond within 30 days.

9. Your Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have these rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to Know: Request details about data we've collected about you in the past 12 months
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of your personal information
  • Right to Opt-Out: Opt out of the "sale" of personal information (note: we do not sell data)
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: You will not receive discriminatory treatment for exercising your rights

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@law-trust.com. We will verify your identity and respond within 45 days.

10. Children's Privacy

Law-Trust.com is not directed to individuals under 18. We do not knowingly collect data from children. If we discover we've collected data from a child, we will delete it immediately.

11. International Data Transfers

Your data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your country of residence (including the United States for Google Analytics). We ensure adequate safeguards are in place for such transfers.

12. Do Not Track Signals

We currently do not respond to Do Not Track (DNT) browser signals. You can control tracking through cookie settings and the Google Analytics opt-out tool.

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date. Significant changes will be communicated via a notice on our homepage.
