Frequently Asked Questions

Is an online will legally valid in the UK?

Yes. A will is legally valid in England, Wales and Scotland provided it is signed in the presence of two independent witnesses who also sign. LegalWills.co.uk provides clear instructions specific to your region.

What is the difference between a will and a living will in the UK?

A last will and testament distributes your assets after death. A living will (Advance Decision) documents your healthcare preferences in case you lose mental capacity. Both are legally recognised in the UK.

Do I need a solicitor to write a will in the UK?

No — you are not legally required to use a solicitor. LegalWills.co.uk documents are reviewed by qualified solicitors and valid across England, Wales and Scotland, at a fraction of the price.

Can I update my will after completing it?

Yes — updates are free for life. Life events such as marriage, divorce, new children, or property changes won't require you to repurchase.

Does Scottish law differ from English law for wills?

Yes — Scottish succession law has key differences, including "prior rights" for spouses and children. LegalWills.co.uk generates documents tailored to Scottish law when you select Scotland as your region.
