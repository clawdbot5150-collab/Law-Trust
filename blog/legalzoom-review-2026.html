LegalZoom Pricing in 2026

LegalZoom's pricing is more complex than competitors — with standalone plans, bundles, and subscription options:

Estate Planning Standalone

  • Basic Will: $89
  • Comprehensive Will (with POA + healthcare directive): $179
  • Living Trust (individual): $279
  • Living Trust (couples): $399
  • Estate Plan Bundle (will + trust + POA + directive): $499–$599

Legal Advantage Subscription

  • Monthly: $34.99/month
  • Annual: $359.88/year ($29.99/month effective)

The subscription includes unlimited 30-minute attorney consultations, document discounts, and access to their full library of legal forms. If you need ongoing legal coverage across multiple areas, this is genuinely competitive.

Hidden Cost Warning

LegalZoom's initial pricing can appear low, but they upsell aggressively — attorney review, notarization services, storage, and updates all cost extra. A basic $89 will can easily turn into $200+ with add-ons. Read the pricing carefully before checkout.

LegalZoom's Attorney Network: The Real Differentiator

Here's where LegalZoom genuinely stands apart: their attorney network. The Legal Advantage subscription gives you access to licensed attorneys in covered practice areas via chat or phone, with unlimited 30-minute consultations.

This is valuable if you have questions like:

  • "Should I use a trust or a will for my specific estate?"
  • "What happens to my rental property in a revocable living trust?"
  • "My family situation is complicated — what estate plan structure do I need?"
  • "Does my small business need to be addressed in my estate plan?"

No other major online estate planning service offers this level of attorney access at a comparable price point. If you have questions that go beyond form-filling, LegalZoom's subscription plan delivers real value.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does LegalZoom cost in 2026?
LegalZoom's estate planning prices range from $89 for a basic will to $599 for a comprehensive estate plan bundle. Their Legal Advantage subscription costs $34.99/month ($359.88/year) and includes attorney consultations and document discounts. Watch for upsells — their base pricing can rise significantly with add-ons like attorney review, notarization, and document storage.
Is LegalZoom worth it in 2026?
LegalZoom is worth it if you want attorney access alongside self-service documents, or if you have legal needs beyond estate planning (business, real estate, family law). It's not the best choice for pure estate planning — Trust & Will offers a better experience at a lower price for wills and trusts. LegalZoom's main advantage in 2026 is their attorney network, which no competitor matches.
Does LegalZoom include attorney consultations?
Yes, with their Legal Advantage subscription ($34.99/month). This includes unlimited 30-minute consultations with licensed attorneys in covered practice areas. Basic standalone document purchases don't include attorney access — you'd pay extra for that. The subscription is the best way to access LegalZoom's attorney network cost-effectively.
How does LegalZoom compare to Trust & Will?
Trust & Will is better for estate planning specifically — more guided, cleaner experience, lower cost, and free lifetime updates. LegalZoom is better for people who want ongoing legal coverage across multiple areas and are willing to pay for attorney access. Both produce legally valid, state-specific documents. Choose Trust & Will for a focused estate plan; choose LegalZoom for broader legal coverage.
Is LegalZoom a real law firm?
No — LegalZoom is a legal technology company, not a law firm. They provide self-help legal documents and connect users with independent attorneys through their network. The platform doesn't create attorney-client relationships directly, though the attorneys in their network are licensed and do create such relationships when you consult them. LegalZoom documents are legally valid when properly executed.
