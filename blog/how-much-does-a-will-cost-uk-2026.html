What Drives Up Will Costs?

Not all wills are equal. Here are the main factors that increase the cost of a solicitor-prepared will:

1. Trusts

Incorporating a trust into your will — whether a discretionary trust, a life interest trust, or a trust for a disabled beneficiary — significantly increases drafting complexity and therefore cost. Trusts often add £300–£1,000+ to the base will fee.

2. Business interests

If you own shares in a company, a partnership interest, or a sole trade business, your will needs to address these carefully, particularly regarding Business Property Relief (BPR) for IHT purposes.

3. Foreign assets

Property or investments abroad may require separate wills in each jurisdiction, or at minimum careful coordination between your UK will and local law.

4. Complex family situations

Blended families, children from multiple relationships, estranged relatives, and specific concerns about particular beneficiaries all add complexity and therefore cost.

5. Appointing the solicitor as executor

Some solicitors offer to act as your executor, charging fees from the estate upon your death. These are typically 1%–4% of the estate value — potentially thousands of pounds. Only use a professional executor if you have no trusted personal executor available.

Free and Subsidised Will Services in the UK

Free Wills Month

Free Wills Month runs in October, with a second phase in March. Participating solicitors offer free basic wills to people aged 55 and over, in exchange for considering a charitable legacy. You are not obliged to leave anything to charity. Check freewillsmonth.org.uk for participating firms.

Will Aid

Will Aid runs every November. Participating solicitors charge a suggested minimum donation of £100 for a single will or £180 for mirror wills, with proceeds going to charity. The resulting will is a full solicitor-prepared document. See willaid.org.uk.

National Free Wills Network

Available year-round for over-55s making a charitable legacy, via various charities' partnership schemes.

LPA Costs in Detail

LPAs have a fixed OPG registration fee of £82 per LPA regardless of how you create them. The variable is the professional or service fee on top:

  • DIY (OPG forms only): £82 per LPA = £164 total. The forms are free to download. You must complete them yourself and ensure they are correctly signed and certified.
  • Online service: £50–£120 service fee + £82 OPG fee per LPA = £132–£202 per LPA. The service guides you through completion and checks for errors before submission.
  • Solicitor: £300–£600 professional fee + £82 OPG fee per LPA = £382–£682 per LPA. The solicitor acts as certificate provider, saving one step.

Fee reductions: People with gross annual income below £12,000 pay a 50% fee reduction (£41 per LPA). Those receiving certain means-tested benefits may be fully exempt. Apply for remission on form LPA120.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

When using a solicitor, watch for these additional charges that can be added to the base fee:

  • VAT: Solicitor fees are usually quoted excluding VAT. Add 20%.
  • Disbursements: Registration fees, copying costs, postage
  • Complex estate charges: Hourly rates for additional discussions or revisions
  • Storage fees: Some firms charge annual or one-time fees to store your will
  • Executor fees: If the solicitor acts as executor, fees are charged from the estate on death

Online services typically charge a flat fee with no hidden extras. Always check whether updates are included or charged separately.

The True Cost of Not Having a Will

Consider these costs of dying intestate:

  • Intestate administration: More complex and often slower than probate with a will. Professional administrator fees may be higher.
  • Family disputes: Legal challenges to intestate estates can cost tens of thousands
  • Court of Protection: If you lose capacity without an LPA, family must apply for deputyship at £3,000–£5,000+
  • Missed IHT planning: A simple will with a nil-rate band trust could save your estate 40% IHT on assets over the threshold

Against these potential costs, even a solicitor's fee of £300 for a simple will represents extraordinary value.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a solicitor charge for a simple will in the UK?
A solicitor typically charges £150–£300 for a straightforward single will in the UK, and £250–£500 for mirror wills for couples. In London and the South East, expect fees at the higher end.
Can I write a free will in the UK?
Free Will Week and Free Wills Month (for over-55s) allow you to have a will written by a participating solicitor for free in exchange for considering a charitable legacy. The Will Aid scheme charges a suggested donation of £100 for a single will.
What makes a will more expensive?
Factors that increase will costs include: trusts, business property, foreign assets, complex family situations, Inheritance Tax planning, multiple properties, and requesting the solicitor to also act as executor.
How much do LPAs cost in the UK in 2026?
The OPG registration fee is £82 per LPA, so £164 for both types. Online preparation costs around £50–£120 per LPA. Using a solicitor adds £300–£600 per LPA. Total cost for both LPAs with a solicitor can be £700–£1,400.
Is it worth paying for an expensive solicitor will?
For simple estates, an online will (£29–£90) provides the same legal validity as a solicitor will. For complex estates — business assets, large IHT liability, disabled beneficiaries, offshore assets — a solicitor's expertise is genuinely valuable and the higher fee is justified.
