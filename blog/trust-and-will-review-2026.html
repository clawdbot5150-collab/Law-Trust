The User Experience: Our Honest Assessment

Trust & Will's onboarding is exceptional. You start with a brief questionnaire that helps them recommend the right plan — will or trust — based on your estate size, family situation, and goals. This is genuinely useful, not just a sales funnel.

The document creation process is clean and intuitive. Questions are phrased in plain English, each accompanied by a clear explanation of why it matters. Unlike some competitors where you're left guessing what "per stirpes distribution" means, Trust & Will explains every concept in terms a non-lawyer can understand.

The process takes approximately 20–45 minutes for a will plan and 45–90 minutes for a trust plan. You can save and return to your draft at any time.

One standout feature: the funding checklist included with trust plans. This is a comprehensive guide to transferring your assets into the trust — the step most people miss. It includes specific instructions for retitling real estate, transferring bank accounts, updating retirement account beneficiaries, and more.

The savings are substantial. For the most common use case — a married couple wanting a complete estate plan with living trust — Trust & Will at $499 saves $2,000–$4,500 compared to an attorney. Lifetime free updates add even more value since attorney amendments typically cost $200–$500 per change.

The one legitimate pricing concern: LawDepot's subscription starts at $9.95/month and includes comparable trust documents. If you only need a simple will or a few documents and don't want a subscription, Trust & Will's one-time pricing is clean and predictable. If ongoing document access matters, LawDepot may offer better value.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Trust & Will cost in 2026?
Trust & Will offers one-time pricing: Will Plan ($199 individual / $299 couples), Trust Plan ($399 individual / $499 couples), and Guardianship Plan ($249). All plans include free lifetime updates — no annual fees or subscription required. Occasional discounts of 10–20% are available; check their website for current promotions.
Is Trust & Will legitimate and legally valid?
Yes. Trust & Will is a well-established, legitimate estate planning platform founded in 2017 with over 500,000 customers. Their documents are attorney-reviewed and state-specific. They're legally valid in all 50 states when properly executed — signed and notarized as required by your state. They're also accredited by the Better Business Bureau and backed by positive reviews across Trustpilot and other platforms.
Does Trust & Will offer attorney access?
Trust & Will's standard plans include attorney-reviewed templates but not unlimited attorney consultations. They offer access to their attorney network for additional questions at extra cost. For included attorney access, LegalZoom's subscription plans or Rocket Lawyer's subscription provide better value. Trust & Will is best for people comfortable with a guided, questionnaire-based experience.
Can I update my Trust & Will documents after purchase?
Yes — all Trust & Will plans include free lifetime updates. You can log back in at any time to update beneficiaries, add assets, change your successor trustee, or modify any part of your plan. There's no additional charge for updates, and they'll generate new document versions reflecting your changes. This is a significant advantage over services that charge for amendments.
How does Trust & Will compare to LegalZoom?
Trust & Will specializes exclusively in estate planning with a more streamlined experience and lower price points. LegalZoom covers all legal documents and includes attorney access in subscription plans. Trust & Will is better for focused estate planning at a one-time price; LegalZoom is better if you want ongoing legal support across multiple areas of your life. Both produce legally valid documents.
