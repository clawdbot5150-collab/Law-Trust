Who Should Choose Trust & Will

Best for →

Trust & Will: The Smart Choice for Most Families

  • You want a complete estate plan (will + healthcare directive + POA) in one bundle
  • You expect to make updates as your life changes — unlimited revisions are free
  • Ease of use is a priority — Trust & Will's interface is the cleanest in the industry
  • You want a living trust option without paying attorney fees ($199 vs $1,500+)
  • You're US-based with a relatively straightforward estate

Trust & Will wins on value. At $69 for a full estate plan package, it's hard to beat. The interface walks you through each section with clear explanations, and the unlimited update policy means you don't have to worry about the service becoming a money pit over time.

Who Should Choose LegalZoom

Best for →

LegalZoom: The Safe Bet for Complex Situations

  • You want attorney review built into your plan (available in higher tiers)
  • You need UK coverage alongside US documents
  • You want a 60-day money-back guarantee for peace of mind
  • You need business formation documents alongside personal estate planning
  • Brand trust matters to you — LegalZoom has been around since 2001

LegalZoom's extra cost is justified when your estate is more complex. The attorney review option, bundled in higher-tier plans, gives you a real legal professional checking your documents — something Trust & Will doesn't include by default.

Pricing Breakdown: What You Actually Pay

  • Will Plan: $69 — includes will, healthcare directive, and financial POA
  • Trust Plan: $199 — includes revocable living trust + all will plan documents
  • Updates: Free, unlimited

LegalZoom Pricing (2026)

  • Basic Will: $99
  • Will + Attorney Review: $179–$249 depending on tier

For a basic will with standard documents, Trust & Will is clearly cheaper — $69 vs $99 at minimum, with LegalZoom's real value kicking in at the higher tiers that include attorney access.

Document Quality

Both services produce attorney-drafted, state-specific documents. The key difference is depth of questioning. LegalZoom's intake process is notably more thorough — it asks about every contingency. Trust & Will is faster and asks fewer questions, which some people prefer but occasionally misses edge cases for complex estates.

For straightforward estates (married couple, a home, standard beneficiaries), both produce equivalent documents. For complex situations, LegalZoom's thoroughness is an advantage.

The Verdict

Choose Trust & Will if: You want the best value, cleanest experience, and unlimited updates for a standard estate.

Choose LegalZoom if: You want attorney review, need UK coverage, or have a more complex estate and want the safety net of a 60-day guarantee.

Both are legitimate, reliable services. You can't go wrong with either — the question is just which is right for your situation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Trust & Will or LegalZoom more trustworthy?
Both are legitimate, attorney-reviewed platforms. LegalZoom has a longer track record (since 2001) and stronger brand recognition. Trust & Will is newer (founded 2017) but has grown rapidly and receives consistently high customer satisfaction ratings.
Does LegalZoom's 60-day guarantee actually work?
Yes — LegalZoom's satisfaction guarantee is real. If you're not happy with your documents within 60 days, you can request a full refund. Customer reviews generally confirm this policy is honored without hassle.
Which is better for a married couple with kids?
Trust & Will's will plan at $69 covers both spouses and includes guardian designation for minor children. For most married couples with standard estates, Trust & Will provides everything needed at a lower cost than LegalZoom.
Can I switch from LegalZoom to Trust & Will later?
Yes. A will created with one service can be superseded (legally replaced) by a new will created with another service. You'd simply execute a new, properly signed will that states it revokes all prior wills. Consult your state's requirements for proper execution.
