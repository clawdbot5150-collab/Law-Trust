Where LawDepot Wins

LawDepot is the better choice when…

You Need Volume and Variety

  • You're a landlord — LawDepot's lease agreements are best-in-class and state-specific
  • You need business documents alongside personal ones (NDAs, contractor agreements)
  • You want to create and cancel after one month ($9.95 total)
  • You're in Canada or Australia (better non-US document coverage)
  • You need multiple document types at the lowest possible cost

LawDepot's real advantage is economics. At $9.95/month, creating a will, POA, healthcare directive, and lease agreement costs you one subscription — vs. $99+ per document at LegalZoom. If you need 3+ documents, LawDepot wins on price every single time.

Where LegalZoom Wins

LegalZoom is the better choice when…

You Need Attorney Oversight

  • You want an attorney to review your documents before signing
  • You need a living trust (LawDepot doesn't offer this)
  • Brand recognition and longevity matter to you (LegalZoom since 2001)
  • You want a 60-day money-back guarantee
  • Your estate is complex and you want more comprehensive estate planning support

LegalZoom's attorney access is the key differentiator. For complex situations where you need a real lawyer to verify your documents — blended families, large estates, contested situations — LegalZoom's optional attorney review is worth the higher price. LawDepot simply doesn't offer this.

Pricing Deep Dive

Creating a Basic Will: LawDepot vs LegalZoom

  • LawDepot: Create and download within the 7-day free trial → $0. Or pay $9.95 for one month.
  • LegalZoom: $99 for a basic will, $179–$249 for will + attorney review tier.

Creating a Will + POA + Lease Agreement: LawDepot vs LegalZoom

  • LawDepot: $9.95 for one month — all three documents included.
  • LegalZoom: $99 (will) + $39 (POA) + separate lease product = $150–$200+.

The math heavily favors LawDepot for multi-document needs. LegalZoom is better value only when you specifically need attorney review or a living trust.

Document Quality Compared

Wills

LegalZoom's will questionnaire is more thorough and covers more edge cases. LawDepot's will is solid and legally valid, but the interface and depth of questioning is less comprehensive. For a basic will, both are adequate. For complex estates, LegalZoom is better.

Lease Agreements

LawDepot wins decisively. Their lease templates are state-specific, comprehensive, and include clauses that LegalZoom's limited real estate offering misses. For landlords, this isn't close.

Business Documents

LawDepot wins on volume. They offer 50+ business document types. LegalZoom focuses more on business formation (LLC, incorporation) than ongoing business contracts. Different strengths for different needs.

The Verdict

There's no universal winner — these services target different primary use cases:

Choose LawDepot if you want maximum document variety at the lowest price, especially for real estate, business, or mixed document needs.

Choose LegalZoom if you need attorney oversight, a living trust, or you want the backing of the most established name in online legal services.

Many users actually benefit from using both — LegalZoom for estate planning (where attorney review matters) and LawDepot for everything else (where volume and price matter).

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better for a landlord — LawDepot or LegalZoom?
LawDepot, without question. Their state-specific lease agreements are comprehensive and regularly updated. LegalZoom's real estate document offering is limited by comparison. For landlords, LawDepot's $9.95/mo subscription is one of the best value propositions in the legal document space.
Can I use both LawDepot and LegalZoom?
Yes, and for complex situations it makes sense. Use LegalZoom for your will and trust (where attorney review matters), and LawDepot for lease agreements, NDAs, and other business documents where volume and price matter more than legal oversight.
Does LegalZoom have a subscription like LawDepot?
LegalZoom offers subscription plans for ongoing legal support (attorney consultations, document storage), but their core document creation is priced per-document. LawDepot's subscription model — unlimited documents for one flat monthly fee — is more economical if you need multiple documents.
Is LawDepot safe for sensitive legal documents?
Yes. LawDepot uses industry-standard encryption and has been in operation since 2001. Your documents are stored securely and can be downloaded in PDF or Word format. They do not share your document content with third parties.
