Two services have carved out a niche specifically for families: Trust & Will (the estate planning specialist) and Fabric by Gerber (the service that combines estate planning with life insurance). Here's how they compare in 2026.

Quick Verdict

🏆 The Bottom Line

Choose Trust & Will if you want the most comprehensive estate planning documents — especially if you need a living trust, couples' wills, or more complex estate structures. Their documents are thorough and specifically designed for estate planning.

Choose Fabric if you're a new parent who wants to quickly create a basic will and get life insurance in one place. Fabric's free will tier and Gerber Life integration make it uniquely appealing for young families on a budget.

Trust & Will: The Estate Planning Specialist

Trust & Will launched in 2017 with a clear focus: make professional-grade estate planning accessible online. In 2026 they remain one of the most-recommended services for individuals and couples who want thorough, state-specific estate planning documents.

What You Get with Trust & Will

Trust & Will offers three main packages:

  • Individual Will Package ($89): Last will and testament, financial power of attorney, healthcare directive, HIPAA authorization
  • Couples' Will Package ($149): All the above for two partners — the best value in this category
  • Living Trust Package ($199): Revocable living trust, pour-over will, certificate of trust, plus all healthcare documents

The couples' package is particularly noteworthy — having both partners' wills done simultaneously for $149 is significantly cheaper than creating two individual wills separately. For married couples and domestic partners, this is the best estate planning value we've found.

Trust & Will's Strengths

  • Living trust option is comprehensive — few competitors offer this online
  • State-specific document requirements are baked in automatically
  • Optional attorney review available as an upgrade
  • Trusted by over 500,000 families

Fabric by Gerber: Estate Planning Meets Life Insurance

Fabric is unique in the online legal services world: it's built by a financial services company (Gerber Life) and combines estate planning with term life insurance in a single app. This approach makes it particularly compelling for young parents who need both.

What Fabric Offers

  • Free Basic Will: A legitimate last will and testament, genuinely free with no hidden fees
  • Guardianship Designations: Specifically designed to help parents designate guardians quickly
  • Healthcare Documents: Living will and power of attorney included
  • Term Life Insurance: Apply for Gerber Life term policies directly within the app
  • Beneficiary Management: Track and update beneficiaries across documents

Fabric's Free Will — Is It Actually Good?

Fabric's free will is a legitimately valid estate planning document, not a limited trial. It's state-specific, covers all the basics (executor, beneficiaries, guardians for children), and can be updated at any time. For simple estates, it does the job well.

The limitations: no living trust, no couples' package, and less depth for complex estates with business ownership, blended families, or significant assets. For those situations, Trust & Will's paid options are clearly superior.

The Life Insurance Angle

Fabric's integration with Gerber Life is its biggest differentiator. You can apply for term life insurance within the same app where you create your will — and the beneficiary information flows between both. For a new parent who needs both a will and life insurance and wants to handle both in one session, Fabric is genuinely convenient.

Which Is Right for You?

Choose Trust & Will if you:

  • Need a living trust (Fabric doesn't offer this)
  • Want to do both partners' wills at once (couples' package is great value)
  • Have a more complex estate — multiple assets, real estate, business interests
  • Want optional attorney review on your documents
  • Need the most thorough healthcare directive and POA options

Choose Fabric if you:

  • Are a new or young parent who needs a basic will quickly
  • Also need life insurance and want to handle both together
  • Want a free option — Fabric's will is genuinely free
  • Prefer a mobile app experience
  • Have a simple estate with straightforward asset distribution

The Price Reality

Fabric wins on price for basic wills — free is hard to beat. But Trust & Will's $149 couples' package often works out better value than two individual Fabric accounts if you have a partner and want more comprehensive documents.

If budget is the primary concern and your estate is simple, start with Fabric's free will to get something in place, then consider upgrading to Trust & Will when your financial picture becomes more complex.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Trust & Will or Fabric better for parents with young children?
Both are excellent for parents, but Fabric by Gerber has an edge for life insurance integration — it lets you purchase term life insurance alongside your will in the same app. Trust & Will is better for comprehensive estate planning including living trusts and couples' plans.
Is Fabric by Gerber free?
Fabric offers a free will creation tool for basic wills. Their premium estate planning documents and life insurance products are paid. The free will option is legitimately useful for simple estates.
How much does Trust & Will cost?
Trust & Will charges $89 for an individual will, $149 for couples' wills (both partners), and $199 for a living trust. These are one-time fees with no subscription required, though annual updates are offered as an add-on.
Does Trust & Will include a living trust?
Yes. Trust & Will's $199 living trust package is one of the most comprehensive online trust creation tools available. It includes a revocable living trust, pour-over will, healthcare directive, and power of attorney documents.
Can I get life insurance through Trust & Will?
Trust & Will does not directly offer life insurance. Fabric by Gerber is the service that uniquely combines estate planning documents with term life insurance through its parent company Gerber Life. This makes Fabric a one-stop shop for new parents specifically.
