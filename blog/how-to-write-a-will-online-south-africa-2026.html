How to Write a Legal Will Online in South Africa 2026

Writing a legally valid will in South Africa requires strict compliance with the Wills Act 7 of 1953 — and the signing formalities are more rigorous than in many other countries. South Africa requires both the testator AND witnesses to sign on every page of the will, making it one of the strictest will execution frameworks in the world.

This step-by-step guide explains exactly what is required to create a valid South African will online, how to correctly execute (sign) it, and the most common mistakes that lead to wills being declared invalid or partially void by the courts or the Master of the High Court.

Why South Africa Has Strict Will Formalities

The Wills Act 7 of 1953's strict requirements reflect South Africa's legal heritage, blending Roman-Dutch law and English common law influences. The page-by-page signing requirement was introduced to prevent fraud — specifically to prevent pages being inserted or removed from a will after it has been signed.

South Africa does not recognise holographic (handwritten, unwitnessed) wills — unlike the UK, Canada, or many US states. This is an important distinction: if you write a handwritten will without witnesses in South Africa, it has no legal validity whatsoever.

The Wills Act 7 of 1953: Core Requirements

Under the Wills Act 7 of 1953, a will is valid if:

  1. It is in writing (typed, printed, or handwritten — but must be witnessed)
  2. It is signed by the testator at the end of the will
  3. The testator signs every other page of the will (not just the last page)
  4. Two competent witnesses (see below) sign at the end of the will in the presence of the testator and each other
  5. Both witnesses must initial every other page of the will (other than the page with the end signature)
  6. The testator must be at least 16 years of age
  7. The testator must have testamentary capacity (understand what they are doing)

Who is a "competent witness"?

Under the Wills Act, a competent witness must be:

  • At least 14 years of age
  • Not a beneficiary under the will (or their spouse)
  • Present at the same time as each other when the testator signs
⚠️ Critical Warning: Section 4A of the Wills Act provides that a person who witnesses a will cannot benefit from it. If you name a beneficiary as a witness, that beneficiary forfeits their inheritance — but the rest of the will remains valid. Always use completely independent witnesses.

Step-by-Step: Creating and Executing Your SA Will

Step 1: Gather your information

Full name, ID number, address, executor details, beneficiary details (full names and ID numbers), specific bequests, guardian for minor children, and your intentions for the residue of your estate.

Step 2: Create your will online

Use LegalWills.co.za to complete the guided questionnaire. The service generates a properly formatted document compliant with the Wills Act 7 of 1953.

Step 3: Print on white A4 paper

Print the completed will on plain white A4 paper. Do not staple the pages — this can complicate the page-by-page signing process. Number all pages clearly.

Step 4: Arrange two independent witnesses

Arrange for two independent adults (not beneficiaries) to be present simultaneously when you sign. Good witnesses: colleagues, neighbours, friends who are not named in the will.

Step 5: Sign in the correct sequence

In front of both witnesses, simultaneously:
• Sign at the END of the will (last page)
• Sign or initial every other page
Both witnesses must also sign at the END and initial every other page, in your presence and each other's presence.

Step 6: Store safely

Store the original will in a secure location. Options include: a bank safety deposit box, your attorney's strongroom, the Deeds Office, or with LegalWills.co.za's secure online storage. Ensure your executor knows where the original is.

What to Include in Your South African Will

Revocation clause

A statement revoking all previous wills. Online services include this automatically.

Executor appointment

Name a personal executor — ideally a trusted family member or close friend. This can save enormous costs versus using a bank trust company (which charges 3.5%+VAT of the gross estate). Include authority for the executor to appoint an attorney as agent.

Testamentary guardian

If you have minor children, appoint a testamentary guardian to care for them if both parents die. The High Court must approve, but places significant weight on your nomination.

Testamentary trust for minor beneficiaries

A testamentary trust in your will holds a minor's inheritance until they reach a specified age (commonly 18, 21, or 25). Without a trust, the Guardian's Fund (administered by the Master of the High Court) holds the minor's inheritance until they turn 18.

Specific bequests

Leave specific assets (jewellery, vehicles, artworks) to named beneficiaries. Describe the asset clearly enough to be identifiable.

Residual estate clause

What happens to everything not specifically mentioned? Typically: to spouse, then to children equally, then to specified substitutes. Always include a complete residual clause.

Special Considerations: Accrual System for Married Couples

Many South African marriages are conducted out of community of property with the accrual system. Under the Matrimonial Property Act 88 of 1984, when a marriage subject to accrual is dissolved (including by death), the spouse whose estate shows the smaller accrual has a claim against the other spouse's estate for half the difference.

This means your estate planning must account for potential accrual claims — your executor needs to calculate and settle any accrual claim before distributing the estate. Ensure your will acknowledges this and gives your executor the necessary authority.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the requirements for a valid will in South Africa?
Under the Wills Act 7 of 1953, a valid South African will must be in writing, signed at the end and on every other page by the testator, and witnessed by two competent witnesses who are both present at the same time. Witnesses must be 14 or older and cannot be beneficiaries or their spouses.
Are holographic (handwritten unwitnessed) wills valid in South Africa?
No. South Africa does not recognise holographic wills. All wills must be witnessed by two competent witnesses under the Wills Act 7 of 1953. A handwritten will that is not witnessed is invalid.
Can beneficiaries witness a will in South Africa?
No. Section 4A of the Wills Act provides that a person who signs a will as a witness cannot benefit from that will. If a beneficiary witnesses the will, their gift fails — but the rest of the will remains valid. Always use completely independent witnesses.
What is the role of the Master of the High Court in South Africa?
The Master of the High Court oversees the administration of deceased estates in South Africa. When someone dies, the estate must be reported to the Master, who issues Letters of Executorship to the appointed executor. The Master also approves the final liquidation and distribution account before assets are distributed.
Must every page of a South African will be signed?
Yes. Under the Wills Act 7 of 1953, the testator must sign at the end of the will and must also sign every other page. Both witnesses must initial every page other than the page bearing the end signature. Missing a page signature can render provisions invalid.
