What LawDepot Actually Offers

LawDepot launched in 2001 and has grown into one of the largest legal document libraries available online. Their 400+ document templates span six major categories:

  • Estate Planning: Last will and testament, living will, power of attorney, living trust
  • Real Estate: Lease agreements, quitclaim deeds, purchase agreements, sublease agreements
  • Business: Partnership agreements, non-disclosure agreements, independent contractor agreements, employment contracts
  • Finance & Debt: Promissory notes, loan agreements, bill of sale
  • Family: Prenuptial agreements, child custody agreements, divorce settlement agreements
  • Personal: Personal loan agreements, demand letters, authorization letters

This is where LawDepot genuinely stands apart. No other service in our comparison covers this range at this price point. If you need a will and a lease agreement and an NDA for a freelance project — LawDepot is the only service that covers all three.

Document Quality: What We Found

LawDepot uses questionnaire-driven document generation — you answer questions, the system builds your document. The quality varies by category:

Estate Planning (Will, POA, Healthcare Directive)

Solid. State-specific requirements are correctly applied. The will questionnaire covers all standard elements: executor, beneficiaries, guardianship for minor children, and residual estate. It's not as polished as Trust & Will's interface, but the output documents are legally sound.

Real Estate (Lease Agreements)

Excellent. This is arguably LawDepot's strongest category. Their lease agreements are comprehensive, state-specific, and cover clauses that other services miss (pet policies, habitability standards, security deposit rules by state). Landlords in particular will find this category exceptional.

Business Documents (NDAs, Contracts)

Good for standard situations. NDAs and independent contractor agreements are straightforward and well-drafted. For complex business structures or high-value contracts, we'd still recommend a business attorney — but for standard small business needs, LawDepot handles these well.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is LawDepot legitimate and legally valid?
Yes. LawDepot has been operating since 2001 and their documents are drafted by licensed attorneys. Documents are state-specific for US users and province-specific for Canadian users. As with any online legal service, the documents are only valid when properly executed (correct signatures and witnesses per your state's requirements).
Can I cancel LawDepot after one month?
Yes. LawDepot's monthly plan has no commitment — cancel anytime before your next billing date. Many users create all their documents in a single month and cancel. You keep your downloaded documents even after cancellation.
Does LawDepot offer a living trust?
No — this is LawDepot's notable gap. They offer a last will and testament, power of attorney, and healthcare directive, but not a revocable living trust. If you need a living trust, Trust & Will ($199) or LegalZoom are better options.
Is LawDepot available outside the US?
Yes. LawDepot covers the United States (all 50 states), Canada (all provinces), the United Kingdom, and Australia. Their Canadian and UK document libraries are particularly strong — making them a top choice for non-US users who find most competitors US-only.
How does LawDepot compare to just Googling a template?
Free templates online are generic, not state-specific, and often outdated. LawDepot's documents are attorney-drafted, jurisdiction-specific, and kept current. For a $9.95/mo subscription (or a single free trial month), the quality difference is significant — especially for documents like leases where state-specific clauses matter legally.
